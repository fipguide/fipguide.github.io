## FIP Information

In Serbia, trains of [Serbian Railways SV](/operator/sv "SV") can be used with FIP Tickets. SV is one of the most important means of transport and operates the majority of passenger traffic in the country.

## Interesting

Serbia has a comparatively extensive rail network of around 3,700 km.

City names and stations are written in Serbian in the timetables and at the stations – since the Cyrillic alphabet is used, this can pose challenges for orientation, especially for travellers who are not familiar with the Cyrillic alphabet. It is advisable to familiarise yourself with the Cyrillic names before travelling or to use a translation app.

The trains in Serbia are predominantly older and less modern than in Western Europe. However, there are increasing efforts to modernise and improve the infrastructure. Several new lines are planned and some are already under construction – for example the high-speed line between Belgrade and Budapest. Some new vehicles from Stadler Rail, CRRC and CAF have also been ordered. The punctuality of services varies and the frequency on most routes is less dense than in Western Europe.

### Hungary

Coming from Budapest, it is possible to reach Belgrade with two changes (Szeged and Subotica). The cross-border section from Szeged in Hungary to Subotica in Serbia is operated as regional traffic. The services are operated by Hungarian Railways MÁV and by SV in Serbia. For cross-border journeys, a through FIP 50 Ticket or FIP Coupons of both railway companies is required.

### Romania

There are currently no passenger trains between Serbia and Romania.

### Bulgaria

At present, only the private Optima Express crosses the border between Serbia and Bulgaria once a week. Boarding and alighting is not possible in either Bulgaria or Serbia.

### North Macedonia

There are currently no passenger trains between Serbia and North Macedonia.

### Kosovo

There are currently no passenger trains between Serbia and Kosovo.

**Note:** Kosovo is not recognised by Serbia as an independent state, therefore Serbia still lays claim to the rail network in Kosovo. Since no cross-border connections exist between Serbia and Kosovo due to the political situation, there is therefore no relevance in the context of FIP.

### Montenegro

From Montenegro, the cross-border connection via Bijelo Polje can be used. A night train runs here daily as well as seasonal daytime trains, which are operated by Montenegrin ŽPCG and Serbian SV.

### Bosnia and Herzegovina

There are currently no passenger trains between Serbia and Bosnia and Herzegovina.

### Croatia

There are currently no passenger trains between Serbia and Croatia.
