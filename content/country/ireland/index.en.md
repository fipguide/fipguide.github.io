## FIP Information

The railway network in Ireland is operated by the state railway company [Irish Rail](/operator/cie) (Iarnród Éireann). FIP discounts apply to all Irish Rail trains.

{{< identify-operator sources="" >}}
With the exception of special services, Irish Rail is the only operator of regular train services in Ireland. Only on the Enterprise Service does the Northern Irish railway company [NIR](/operator/nir) take over operations on the section between Dundalk and Belfast.
{{< /identify-operator >}}

## Interesting

The railway network in Ireland is radially oriented around Dublin, from where connections lead to many regions of the country. The frequency between larger cities is generally good. In more rural regions, frequencies are sometimes significantly lower.

Reservations are not mandatory, but are recommended due to the often high occupancy.

### United Kingdom

There is a direct train connection between Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland between Belfast and Dublin, known as the _Enterprise_, operated by [Irish Rail](/operator/cie) and [Northern Ireland Railways](/operator/nir). Between Dublin and Dundalk, trains can be used with CIE FIP Coupons; between Dundalk and Belfast, NIR FIP Coupons are required. Alternatively, FIP 50 Tickets can be purchased for the entire route.

Besides the Enterprise Service, there are no other cross-border train connections.

From Dublin and Rosslare, Irish Ferries and Stena Line Limited ferries operate to Holyhead and Fishguard in Wales. The ferry connections from [Stena Line Limited](/operator/sll) can be used at a discount with Stena Line Limited FIP Coupons or FIP 50 Tickets.

{{% highlight tip %}}
Stena Line Limited and Irish Ferries offer tickets that allow combined train and ferry travel at a reduced price. No additional FIP discount is available on these tickets.
